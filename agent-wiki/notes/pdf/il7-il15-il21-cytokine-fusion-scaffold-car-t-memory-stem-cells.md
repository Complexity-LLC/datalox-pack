## Evidence Snippets

- We demonstrated that HCW9206, a unique protein scaffold linking interleukin-7 (IL-7), an IL-15/IL-15 receptor α (IL-15Rα) complex, and IL-21, generates CAR T cells without requiring αCD3/28 activation, which are highly enriched in long-lived T memory stem cells (TSCM cells) (>50%) and display potent activity across distinct disease models, HIV-1 or B cell leukemia.
- In a humanized mouse HIV infection model, HCW9206-generated anti-HIV duoCAR T cells suppressed viremia more effectively than αCD3/28-generated anti-HIV duoCAR T cells.
- In a xenograft leukemia mouse model, a recall proliferative response and complete clearance of leukemia rechallenge were displayed by HCW9206-generated but not by αCD3/28-generated antiCD19 CAR T cells.
- HCW9206, a first-in-class cytokine scaffold–based platform, enables production of more potent CAR T cell–based immunotherapies by generating a CAR T cell population, which is highly functional and also markedly enriched for long-lived TSCM cells.
- This strategy is broadly
- Together, these data demonstrate that treating T cells with a combination of IL-7, IL-15SA, and IL-21 cytokines delivered by a single biologic, HCW9206, can quickly and effectively produce HIVand CD19-specific CAR T cells that are highly enriched for the TSCM cell memory phenotype, as well as effector T cells capable of maintaining suppression of HIV and leukemic cell proliferation.
- Therefore, generating CAR T cells through HCW9206 treatment could provide a new, improved, and highly scalable method for generating CAR T cells to treat patients with cancer and infectious diseases and replace standard CAR T cell production using αCD3/28 activation.
- This has widespread implications for the generation of more robust CAR T cell–based immunotherapies to improve CAR T cell functional persistence and efficacy for the treatment of HIV and cancer.
